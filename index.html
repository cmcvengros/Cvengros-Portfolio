About Me

Hello! My name is Claire Cvengros and I have recently graduated from Washington University in St. Louis with a Bachelor of Arts in Anthropology and minors in Computer Science and Environmental Studies looking to begin my professional career.

As an Anthropology student, I have always been passionate about how humans live, our differing customs and traditions, and the way we interact with the tools we have created. Whether it be stone tools, written language, or motorized vehicles, humans have continuously created technologies to enhance their quality of life, and computer technologies are no exception. To better understand human culture, I first had to understand their technology. Computers are arguably one of the most influential advancements in human history, and I became addicted to creating new programs and websites for humans to use and studying the way they interact with them. This was the reason I chose to study both Anthropology and Computer Science, as opposite as these fields may seem. In my future career, I strive to continue combining the two - by both studying how humans function, learning and creating new technologies, and how these technologies can advance our culture.

Additionally, I am also very passionate about environmental protection and promoting sustainability in our everyday lives, hence the Environmental Studies minor. I love animals, nature, and travelling, and have spent a lot of my time researching and protecting endangered species in West Africa on behalf of Washington University's Anthropology Department.
